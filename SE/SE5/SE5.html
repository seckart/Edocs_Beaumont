Commitment to Professional Development SE5

 

Nursing education opportunities are provided for those interested in a nursing career.

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, of a career development opportunity provided by the organization for non-nurse employees or members of the community interested in becoming a registered nurse. This example may include community partnership activities.

 

 

Career Development Opportunity Provided by Beaumont Grosse Pointe

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) offers a career development opportunity to students from two local community high schools. Grosse Pointe North High (GPN) is located in the city of Grosse Pointe Woods, approximately two miles from BGP, and Lake Shore High (LSH) is in Saint Clair Shores, approximately nine miles from BGP. LSH also has a cooperative arrangement with Roseville Public School that allows students from Roseville High School to attend LSH’s Health Occupations Program.

 

The purpose of the collaboration with the high schools is to give students the opportunity to observe the many different areas within nursing and the hospital/healthcare setting, thus broadening their knowledge of the various roles and specialties that are available in nursing and other areas of healthcare to increase their interest in nursing careers. BGP’s objective in coordinating with the community high schools is to promote the nursing profession so that students who complete their nursing degree will choose to work at BGP. Although the focus is nursing, the students are also introduced to entry level employment opportunities as, for example, nursing care assistants, clinical assistants and transportation personnel.

 

Participation by Community Members
For the past four years, Debra Briske, MSN-Ed, RN, CEN has coordinated the community partnership between BGP and the two high schools. GPN’s Applied Medical Research Course and LSH’s Health Occupations Course are recognized programs for which participants receive college credit in addition to their high school credit. Although both courses concentrate on healthcare careers in general, the observation at BGP focuses on nursing careers. When students observe an ancillary discipline, the experience is then discussed in terms of how that department interacts with and supports nursing. 

 

Students must pass prerequisite high school courses prior to enrolling in the BGP observation. In addition, the students must have an interest in enrolling in higher education that is nursing or healthcare-based. Students must comply with health regulations, so they are instructed on HIPAA regulations, standard precautions, handwashing and patient confidentiality. The students are also required to be current with their immunizations and TB testing, and they must obtain parental consent on the work agreement to attend the BGP observations.

 

Process
In preparation for the start of the fall semester, Briske contacts the GPN and LSH instructors to ensure that the observation is on the agenda and to find out the number of students that each school has in the program. Once that is established, an email is sent to BGP department leaders requesting their continued support in participating with student observations and asking those who have not previously participated to consider doing so. To ensure that the students have a well-rounded nursing experience, the following nursing departments are contacted: Pre-op, Post-op, Endoscopy, Critical Care, 2NE – Critical Care Step Down, 2SE – Stroke/Hospice, 3SE – Orthopedic, 3W – GI/Surgical, Emergency Center, Dialysis, Interventional Heart and Vascular, Anesthesia and Infusion Services. The students are also given the opportunity to observe in the Radiology, Laboratory, Transportation and Biomedical Engineering departments.

 

BGP leaders and caregivers are involved with and supportive of students, and they readily share their knowledge and resources. A department or nursing unit will occasionally decline to host the high school students due to construction in their department, new hires who are in orientation or possibly new leadership in the department, but as soon as they are able to accommodate the students again the leader will rejoin. All leaders are asked to complete a grid stating whether their department/unit will participate (Y/N) along with dates/times and contact information.  (Evidence SE5-1, High School Observation Grid at BGP Email and Observation Grid)

 

Once the leaders have completed the information on the grid, Briske compiles the observation rotation information into a user friendly table for the schools to use when scheduling the student observations. The rotation table gives information regarding unit, contact person, contact information (phone number and email), day and time availability and other special requests (e.g., observation will have two students in each area unless otherwise noted). (Evidence SE5-2, GPN and LSH High School Observation Rotation 2015-2016)  

 

Before the start of the high school observations, students attend an orientation at BGP that includes didactic presentations on handwashing, BGP standards/professionalism, work/observer agreement, confidentiality, a tour of the hospital and confirming that vaccines are current and that forms are signed. Part of the orientation is dedicated to answering students’ questions and clarifying BGP’s expectations of the observing high school student. It is stressed that this is mostly a nursing experience with the goal of increasing the nursing pool at BGP. (Evidence SE5-3, High School Observation Orientation Agenda)

 

The week before the start of student observations at BGP, instructors from GPN and LSH send to all leaders the schedule showing the date and time that students are assigned to the units so the leaders can prepare. (Evidence SE5-4, Clinical Rotation Schedule) The nursing units typically have the students shadow a registered nurse who is able to explain the particulars of the nurse role in that department/unit and the type of patients who are seen. For example, Annette Gipprich, RN, clinical nurse on 2SE, takes great pride in being a nurse at BGP and shares her enthusiasm with the high school students when they observe on her unit. Departments such as Laboratory and Biomedical Engineering have interactive stations set up and/or provide tours of different job responsibilities to enhance the students’ learning experience.

 

In 2015, 14 LSH students and 49 GPN students participated in the clinical observations at BGP, and over the past four years a total of 228 high school students have participated. The expectation for taking this course and participating in the observation experience is that the students intend to pursue a career in nursing and a degree in healthcare. Of the students who have participated, approximately 90% have been accepted into a healthcare degree program. Approximately 60% of these pursue a nursing degree, while the rest pursue physical therapy, respiratory therapy, occupational therapy or medical school. Approximately 75% of those who enroll in a nursing program complete the degree.

 

Evaluation
BGP often receives cards from individual students demonstrating their appreciation for the experience. Cards or emails may be sent to a particular department/unit, or they may be a global thank you to the hospital as evidenced by emails sent to Briske. (Evidence SE5-5, Thank You Notes) It has been challenging to obtain evaluation data from the schools for various reasons, including student confidentiality and the lack of formal follow-up.

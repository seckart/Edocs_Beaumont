Commitment to Professional Development SE3EO

 

The organization supports nurses' continuous professional development.

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, illustrating that the organization has met a targeted goal for improvement in professional nursing certification. Supporting evidence must be submitted in the form of a graph with a data table that clearly displays the data.
    AND

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, illustrating that nursing has met a targeted goal for improvement in professional nursing certification by unit or division (e.g., cardiac-vascular, gerontological, medical-surgical, nursing informatics, pediatrics, psychiatric-mental health). Supporting evidence must be submitted in the form of a graph with a data table that clearly displays the data.

 

For system applications, refer to system requirements in Appendix C

 

 

Certification of nurses has been a goal of Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) and various nursing departments for a number of years. Nursing certification validates the knowledge gained through professional education and experience, promoting quality patient care. Certified nurses support the concepts of commitment, accountability, credibility and professionalism, and certification demonstrates leadership and strengthens professional and personal pride. Certified nurses are more confident in their clinical abilities, which in turn increases the patient’s confidence in the care they receive. BGP nurses are certified in approximately 35 different specialty areas.

 

Increasing the number of nurses holding certification has become a strategic priority for all levels of BGP nursing, with certification being actively promoted in all areas of the hospital. Nurses at all levels are beginning to talk about certification, and discussions about the benefits of certification are being held.

 

As part of Beaumont Health, BGP nurses are provided numerous review courses to assist with certification. These include:

  • Critical Care Review Course
  • Medical Surgical Review Course
  • Gerontology Review Course
  • Nurse Executive Review Course

 

The hospital supports certification for all nurses in many ways. For example, the Professional Ladder of Excellence for clinical nurses includes an option to use a national certification to meet the criteria for level III, and certification and a BSN are required for clinical nurse level IV.

 

Certification is also supported financially. Nurses are reimbursed up to $400 for the cost of the certification exam, and they can also use up to $500 of their education assistance to support professional development. 

 

On March 21, 2016, BGP recognized 40 certified nurses by holding a certification recognition tea in the private dining room from 2 to 3 p.m. Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer, sent an invitation to the recognition tea to all nurses who had achieved a nursing certification. Stewart attended the celebration and personally offered her congratulations and recognition for their achievement of certification.

 

The Nursing Clinical Integration Council, which comprises BGP’s executive nursing leaders, set a goal at its October 15, 2014 meeting to increase the number of certified nurses in the hospital by 5% per year. The council also set a goal to increase the number of certified nurses in each unit by 5% per year.

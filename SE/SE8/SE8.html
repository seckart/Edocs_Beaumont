Teaching and Role Development SE8

 

The organization provides educational activities to improve the nurse's expertise as a preceptor.

  • Describe the organization's preceptor educational program(s) and how each program is evaluated on an ongoing basis. Provide supporting evidence.

 

 

 

Preceptor Education Program Offered by Beaumont Grosse Pointe

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) participates in a robust Nurse Preceptor Workshop that is offered to experienced nurses who are currently precepting or would like to precept new clinical nurses. This workshop provides nurse preceptors with evidence-based skills to build supportive one-on-one teaching and learning relationships with new nurses. 

 

The workshop was originally developed in 2011with input from Beaumont Grosse Pointe, Beaumont Royal Oak and Beaumont Troy. It is currently taught by the Educational Specialists from Beaumont Royal Oak. At Beaumont Health, the workload is typically split between the three North Hospitals.

 

The need for qualified, experienced, effective preceptors continues to grow as many of BGP’s new hires are new nurse graduates. The goal of the Nurse Preceptor Workshop is that at the end of the program the preceptor will have the essential tools and ideas for practical approaches to facilitate meeting the new nurse’s adult learning style, validate competencies and develop critical feedback and evaluation skills.

 

Objectives of the Nurse Preceptor Workshop

  • Describe the preceptor roles and responsibilities
  • Define communication, critical thinking and emotional intelligence
  • Identify the steps for assessing and planning learning
  • Describe how to evaluate performance and provide feedback
  • Discuss challenging or difficult situations and how to manage conflict
  • Discuss how to develop a learning plan
  • Discuss the transition from preceptor to mentor
  • Identify current hot topics and issues to be included during the orientation period

 

Frequency and Length of the Program
The Nurse Preceptor Workshop is offered monthly at the PNC Building in Troy, Michigan. This is an off-site conference center at which Beaumont Health rents classroom space for the education needs of the employees of Beaumont Grosse Pointe, Beaumont Royal Oak and Beaumont Troy. 

 

The participants of the class are clinical nurses from three Beaumont hospitals: Beaumont Grosse Pointe, Royal Oak, and Troy. The workshop is held from 0800–1400, and 4.5 continuing nurse education contact hours are awarded by the Ohio Nurses Association via the American Nurses Credentialing Center (ANCC) upon completing the class. (Evidence SE8-1, 2017 RN Preceptor Workshop Advertisement and CE Agenda)

Preceptor Selection
Experienced nurses must indicate a desire to be a preceptor, and the manager must give approval that the nurse is a qualified candidate to be permitted into the Nurse Preceptor Workshop. Nurses may approach the manager, or the manager may approach nurses about attending the Nurse Preceptor Workshop. Preceptors are identified in many ways. A manager may identify a potential preceptor by observing the clinical nurse providing patient care, and clinical nurses are frequently identified during their performance review. The manager will then inquire about their interest in precepting. Clinical nurses may also be identified as potential preceptors if they have been a preceptor for a registered nurse student for a local university. Most nursing school in the Detroit area require a precepted experience at a hospital. 

 

Nurses are paid to attend the workshop, and they receive 4.5 contact hours along with a certificate of completion. For clinical nurses participating in the Professional Ladder Program, the Nurse Preceptor Workshop can be used to fulfill requirements of attendance at a leadership course. In the past four years, 68 nurses from BGP attended the Nurse Preceptor Workshop, including 17 in 2016. (Evidence SE8-3, Continuing Education and Attendance List from 2016 Workshops)

 

Ongoing Evaluation of the Nurse Preceptor Workshop
The Nurse Preceptor Workshop is evaluated approximately every two years or more frequently if required. The Clinical Nurse Specialists from all three hospitals and the instructors for the course meet to review the content and make any necessary changes. They review the participants’ evaluations of the class to determine whether it is still meeting the clinical nurses’ needs.

 

The workshop is evaluated in three ways: 

  • All attendees evaluate the workshop after completing it. They are sent an online evaluation that must be completed prior to receiving the CNE certificate. This survey is used to evaluate the instructors’ ability to teach and whether the objectives were met.
  • Every other year, the instructors review the course content for relevance and current evidence-based practice and update it as needed. This course was originally developed in 2011, and it was revised and updated in 2013, 2014 and 2016. (Evidence SE8-4, RN Preceptor Workshop Updates)
  • Ongoing feedback from the monthly evaluation surveys is used to ensure that the workshop is meeting the needs of the preceptors, and adjustments are made as indicated.

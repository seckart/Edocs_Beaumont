SE9

 

Commitment to Community Involvement SE9

 

The organization supports nurses' participation in community healthcare outreach.

  • Provide orovide one example, with supporting evidence, of organizational support for nurse leader involvement in community healthcare outreach.

 

 

Example One: Beaumont Grosse Pointe Clinical Nurses Providing Healthcare Outreach in the Community – Stroke Awareness and Health Screenings

Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) is committed to providing organizational support to nurses who participate in community healthcare outreach.

 

In late 2016,Cathleen Solecki, MSN, RN, CCRN, CNRN, Clinical Program Manager, was contacted by Suzy Berschback, Community Relations Manager for BGP, for organizational support to facilitate clinical nurse involvement in several community outreach events. Solecki agreed to solicit clinical nurses to participate in this program.  Solecki and Berschback agreed that the community outreach would focus on activities related to early detection and prevention for community members who are at risk for strokes.

 

Recruitment of Clinical Nurse Participants
Solecki is in charge of the BGP Stroke Champion program. Stroke Champions are clinical nurses who serve as key stakeholders and function as resources and clinical experts with a strong influence in process and action plans driving the Stroke Program from the bedside. The Stroke Champion role is designed to improve clinical performance, quality of care and process improvement opportunities. One of the requirements of being a Stroke Champion nurse is to participate in at least one community outreach event annually. (Evidence SE9-1, Criteria for Being a Stroke Champion)

 

At the outreach events, the clinical nurse Stroke Champions provide stroke awareness, perform blood pressure checks and ask the participants a series of questions to evaluate their cardiovascular risks for a stroke. These questions cover blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes, smoking, atrial fibrillation, diet, exercise and family history.

 

Outreach events that clinical nurses have participated in include:

  • Power of the Heart – focuses on heart health
  • Girls Night Out – stroke awareness and blood pressure screening
  • Village Fest – stroke awareness and blood pressure screening
  • Stroke Awareness Month – blood pressure and stroke risk assessment

 

Outreach Activity 
Theresa Lopez, RN, clinical nurse, Critical Care Stepdown Unit (CCSU), has been employed by BGP for over seven years and has been an active member of the Stroke Champions.  Working on the CCSU, Lopez has seen many patients have serious health issues related to hypertension and strokes.

 

Lopez is quick to volunteer to participate in any event that is related to hypertension and stroke awareness. One event, held at BGP on May 25, 2017, is the stroke awareness event for Stroke Awareness Month. The event, held in the BGP Lobby from 11 a.m.- 1 p.m., consisted of the Stroke Champions providing blood pressure checks and stroke risk assessments. This event is free to community members. The participants received a FASTER magnet to learn the signs and symptoms of a stroke. FASTER stands for:
F= Face
A= Arm
S= Stability
T= Talking
E= Eyes
R= React

 

On May 17, 2017, Solecki sent a reminder email asking for additional Stroke Champions for the event, and Lopez volunteered. Lopez participated in the stroke risk assessment, which includes questions about a person’s risk factors for stroke. The questions are reviewed and a calculation is made to assess the participant’s risk factors for stroke. This could be low risk, caution or high risk. Based on the areas of risk identified, the clinical nurse reviews ways to reduce their risk of stroke. The signs and symptoms from the National Stroke Association are also reviewed, and the participant is given a magnet with this information. The assessment along with the blood pressure documented on it is also given to the participant. (Evidence SE9-2, Flyer for Stroke Awareness Month, Email Asking for Volunteers, Stroke Risk Scorecard and Copy of Magnet)

 

The event was a huge success, with almost 50 visitors, volunteers and employees being seen during its two hours. Solecki sent an email thanking all the Stroke Champions for participating in the event. (Evidence SE9-3, Thank You Email and Sign-in Sheets from Event)

 

Organizational Support
BGP supports clinical nurses’ involvement in community outreach in many ways. The support is provided by leaders including the Chief Nursing Officer, the Stroke Program Manager and the Administrative Managers on the unit. 

 

Flexible Scheduling and Pay for Volunteering Time at Community Events
Whenever a clinical nurse such as Lopez participates in community outreach, they are paid for the time they are at the event. Administrative Managers support the clinical nurses being compensated for the time they spend doing community outreach. When Lopez participated in the stroke awareness event on May 25, 2017, she was paid for the time she was there.

 

The Administrative Managers also support clinical nurses’ involvement in community outreach by adjusting schedules to ensure they able to attend the outreach activity. (Evidence SE9-4, Email Regarding Paying Clinical Nurses for Outreach, Screen Shot of Lopez Time and Attendance Sheet)

Advancement on Clinical Ladder for Volunteering at Community Events
BGP also supports community outreach through the Clinical Ladder Program. Points may be obtained in the Leadership sections of the Clinical Ladders, with Volunteer Service including:

    • Volunteer Health Related Activities- Not paid by Beaumont
    • Volunteer Community Activities- Sponsored by Beaumont

 

Both activities allow for 1 point per hour, with a maximum of five or 10 points per activity. (Evidence SE9-5, Holly Kowal Professional Ladder Application)

 

Example Two: Beaumont Nurse Leader Marie Reinman Appointed to the Grosse Pointe Chamber of Commerce

 

On January 20, 2015, Suzy Berschback, Community Relations Manager for Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP), contacted Marie Reinman, RN, Director of Heart & Vascular and Critical Care Nursing, regarding a request from the Grosse Pointe Chamber of Commerce for a nursing leader to be a member of the Quality of Life Committee (a subset of the Grosse Pointe Chamber of Commerce). (Evidence SE9-6, Memo from Suzy Berschback)

 

Involvement in Community Outreach
Berschback believed that as a resident of the community of Grosse Pointe Farms and as the Director of Heart & Vascular Services and Critical Care Nursing, serving on the Quality of Life Committee would be a good fit for Reinman, as she could offer input that would have value for community healthcare outreach. Rick Swaine, MBA, MSF, CPA, Hospital President, and Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RN, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer, supported the endeavor, also believing that nurse leader involvement would add value.

 

The objectives of the Quality of Life Committee are to:

  • Create or support opportunities for residents to come together to celebrate the community and support area businesses
  • Promote and encourage a healthy community

 

In addition to BGP nurse leader Reinman, the members of the committee included:

  • Jenny Boettcher, Community Member
  • Carlos Borrego, Community Member
  • Christina Klien, Community Member
  • Dr. David Balle, Physician Community Member
  • Thomas Harwood, Superintendent of the Grosse Pointe School System
  • Tomasine Marx, Vice President of Finance of St. John Health
  • Eric Turin, Attorney
  • Stu Alderman, Director of a local community center
  • Alicia Carlisle, Local business owner

 

Community Healthcare Outreach Activities

Butterfly Bench Project
In 2014-2015, the broader Chamber of Commerce sponsored a Butterfly Bench Project that involved local artists painting benches sponsored by businesses, schools, community organizations and private donors. The benches were auctioned to benefit a local senior charity and remain sprinkled throughout the community

 

10,000 Step Journey
Reinman also supported the chamber’s 10,000 Step Journey program, a way to encourage healthy activity, and tasked the Quality of Life Committee with finding a way to connect this initiative to the Butterfly Bench Project to celebrate Grosse Pointe as a walkable community. Reinman, with the support of BGP, was instrumental in developing a plan to tie together the 10,000 Step Journey walkable community project and the Butterfly Bench Project.

 

Planning for the Walkable Community
At a meeting held on February 10, 2015, the Quality of Life Committee discussed how to tie the healthy activity of walking to the Butterfly Benches Project. They decided to create a small, laminated map marking points of interest and providing mileage between points. They agreed that walks between the benches would be a good starting point for a walking map, which would also focus on the artistry of Grosse Pointe residents.  The committee members with marketing connections agreed to find out what this might cost.

 

At the March 10, 2015 meeting, the group discussed the possibility of incorporating local landmarks and creating different routes based on these; for example, a retail route in the commercial shopping district and routes between schools, churches, clubs and hospitals. The distance between these landmarks was easily calculated, and the benches became points of interest or rest stops on the routes. 

 

Reinman suggested that BGP consider donating pedometers to support the initiative, which it did. The committee started looking at how the map would be distributed to the community members, deciding that this would be done at community events, local newcomers organizations or through the chamber itself. 

 

Since local businesses were highlighted on routes, there was also the possibility of having the cost of the map underwritten based on sponsorships or revenue generated by placing advertisements on the back of the map.

 

The subcommittee continued to meet every other month between May and December 2015, and it further developed the map by adding parks, City Halls of the individual Grosse Pointes and historic sites. (Evidence SE9-7, Quality of Life Meeting Minutes, February 10, 2015, May 12, 2015 and July 14, 2015)

 

A final draft of the map was ready for production by December 2015, complete with sponsorship and enough advertising to pay for printing and laminating the maps. In March 2016, St. John Health offered to provide water bottles since BGP had offered pedometers. 

 

By April 2016, the printed maps were available and planned for distribution at the Chamber of Commerce office, local parks, and fitness and community centers.  Although there was no scientific data to support its success, the committee members heard very positive anecdotal responses to the map. It generated a lot of interest in the routes, and people found it an interesting, innovative way to promote a healthy lifestyle and to promote the community as walking-friendly.

 

Organizational Support
Beaumont Grosse Pointe supports Reinman’s involvement in participation in community outreach.

  • BGP supported Reinman in her position on the Quality of Life Committee by supporting the Walkable Community/Butterfly Bench Project. BGP purchased a butterfly bench that sits in the healing garden on the hospital campus. (Evidence SE9-8, Thank You Letter from Grosse Pointe Chamber Foundation) BGP also purchased pedometers for community members to monitor the number of steps they took while using the walking maps developed by the Quality of Life Committee. (Evidence SE9-9, Thank You Letter from Grosse Pointe Chamber of Commerce, Map and Flyer)
  • BGP supports Reinman for her community outreach by supporting her attendance at the monthly Chamber of Commerce meetings, which are held at 7 a.m., during work time. (Evidence SE9-10, Reinman’s Calendar for February 10, 2015)

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe encourages and supports nurse leaders to participate in community healthcare outreach. BGP’s support for Reinman’s participation on the Chamber of Commerce Quality of Life Committee is one example of this support.

Beaumont Nurses Partner with the Community to Increase Awareness of Breast Cancer in the Grosse Pointes

 

Background/Problem
According to the Michigan Department of Community Health (MDCH), cancer is the second leading cause of death in the state of Michigan, contributing to significant economic and social cost. Uninsured patients and those from ethnic minorities are more likely to be diagnosed with cancer at a later stage, when treatment is more extensive, debilitating and costly. 

 

The focus of Michigan cancer programs is to enhance initiatives, capacity and infrastructures toward the reduction of cancer morbidity and mortality and related health disparities.

 

In 1991, the MDCH implemented a statewide breast and cervical cancer control program. Low income women should have access to lifesaving breast and cervical cancer screening services such as consults and follow-up care, including treatment if necessary.    

 

Based on the Community Assessment Report and a review of the data on the incidence of breast cancer in Michigan, Phyllis Reynolds, MSN, RN, Beaumont Grosse Point (BGP) Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer at the time, and Deborah Kleinhomer, MSN, RN, Administrative Director, BGP, determined that the communities BGP serves had higher rates of breast cancer diagnosis and death than the state of Michigan and the nation. Wayne County is home to two different populations: Detroit is a poor, primarily minority community, and Grosse Pointe is more affluent. Some researchers have speculated that the breast cancer rate is higher in the Grosse Pointes due to all the pesticides used to maintain the lawns and gardens of homes in this area.

 

Due to the higher than average breast cancer rate in BGP’s service area, it wanted to partner with the community to increase the number of women who are consulted and screened at the Comprehensive Breast Care Center. The goal was to increase the number of consults because early diagnosis improves outcomes.

 

Reynolds, Kleinhomer and Robert Kilpatrick, BSRS, RTR, Administrative Director, BGP, were charged with improving the breast care area in the Radiology Department. The goal was to develop a Breast Care Center that is in the Radiology Department, but separate, to maintain the privacy of patients who are experiencing a life changing event.


Reynolds and Kleinhomer met with Suzy Berschback, Community Relations Manager for BGP, to discuss their concern and inquire how BGP could partner with the community to improve breast cancer screening for the community. Berschback suggested they attend a meeting of Beaumont Community Health Alliance, which is a group of 30 community members representing various aspects of the community. Members include representatives from Beaumont Grosse Pointe, Neighborhood Club, Grosse Pointe Chamber of Commerce, Grosse Pointe Public Schools, Harper Woods Public Schools, American Cancer Society and various other community groups.

 

In third quarter 2013, Reynolds and Kleinhomer met with the Health Alliance to discuss the proposed Breast Care Center and the goal of increased breast cancer awareness and the need for increased screening. After the presentation, the group agreed that this would be a good project. 

 

Berschback arranged a focus group to solicit ideas about what an ideal Breast Care Center would look like. The focus group members were chosen based on their prominence in the Grosse Pointe community and their desire to partner with BGP to provide state of the art breast care to the women of the Grosse Pointes and surrounding area. The following community members were asked to sit on the focus group:

  • Mrs. Christopher J. Blake
  • Mrs. Libby Candler
  • Mrs. Hadley French
  • Mrs. Richard Platt
  • Mrs. Sheila Crandall
  • Mrs. Susan Kraft

 

The focus group was facilitated by Kilpatrick and Reynolds. A key strategy identified by the focus group was the need for BGP to hire a nurse who would assist women who come in for diagnostic testing. They believed this nurse would help women navigate the Beaumont systems and help ensure the next steps are followed.

 

In fourth quarter 2013, Bernadine Sherwood, MHSA, BSN, RN, ONC, CBCN, Nurse Navigator, and Mary Smith, BSN, RN, Breast Radiology Nurse, were hired to improve the care of breast cancer patients. A key strategy identified by Sherwood to improve this care was to increase the number of consults to the Breast Care Center to promote early diagnosis.

 

The ad hoc Comprehensive Breast Care Committee was formed to develop and improve the Breast Care Center at BGP.  The committee’s first goal was to improve care, market the importance of breast exams and consults, and make adjustments to the services after the Breast Care Center opened. 

In fourth quarter 2013, there were 60 breast care consults at the Breast Care Center.

 

Goal Statement
To increase the number of breast care consults to the Comprehensive Breast Care Center at Beaumont Grosse Pointe.

 

Description of the Intervention/Initiative/Activities
January 2014
Sherwood participated in the following activities to increase awareness of early diagnosis leading to better outcomes: 

  • Sherwood worked with Chandler Park Academy - Harper Woods Health Fair, held at a local high school. The goal was to increase high school students and parents’ awareness of the importance of breast health and stress the importance of early diagnostic testing.
  • Sherwood held a Breast Center Open House on Sundays following the Catholic Mass in the Hospital Chapel. Tours of the breast Care Center were given and its services explained to educate the public on breast cancer. This event was held in conjunction with church services since many community members attend Catholic Mass at BGP.
  • Sherwood also visited physician offices and clinics to market the Breast Care Center to doctors and their staffs. The goal was to increase community awareness of breast health.

 

February 2014
The official ribbon cutting ceremony for the Breast Care Center was held, with Jennifer Boettcher, Director, Grosse Pointe Chamber of Commerce, to market its services to the community. Boettcher stressed the importance of the community partnering with BGP nurses to address a healthcare need. The Breast Care Center was now a dedicated area, rather than just a sub-department in Radiology.

 

March 2014
BGP’s Community Relations Department offered a community education series at the Grosse Pointe War Memorial to promote women’s health. One of the workshop lectures, “No Excuses,” stressed that there was no excuse to not have a breast exam. Sherwood was present and distributed to all attendees information about the services offered at the new center.  

 

April 2014
“Sharing and Caring Education” night was held in the Connelly Auditorium, with the lecture provided by Adli Yakan, MD, Chief Medical Oncology. The Breast Care Center is adjacent to the Connelly Auditorium, so Sherwood offered tours after the lecture.

 

May 2014
Sherwood and Smith attended a Breast Care Center focus group consisting of patients who had been to the center in the first three months after its opening. The purpose of the focus group was to partner with the community and current patients to develop ways to improve the center.

 

The following suggestions were implemented based on the feedback from the focus group participants:

  • For more privacy a door was added from the changing room to the hallway so patients would no longer need to pass through the gowned waiting area to enter and exit.
  • A private exit was added from the suite to the outside for patients who are distressed.
  • For added privacy the stereotactic exam rooms were relocated from the front of the suite to the back of the suite.
  • For added comfort the hospital gown was changed at both the hospital and in Beaumont Health System.
  • Care will be provided with all caregivers stressing personalized conservation, support and guidance.
  • The Breast Care Center nursing staff will provide courtesy calls, assist with scheduling, facilitate callback appointments and help expedite follow-up with the breast care surgeon within 7-10 days.

 

June 2014
BGP sponsored the Beaumont Golf Outing to benefit the Beaumont Grosse Pointe Comprehensive Breast Care Center. Sherwood was at the event, which was held at the Country Club of Detroit. More than 100 hospital and community members participated in the event, which raised over $80,000 for the BGP Comprehensive Breast Care Center.

Outcomes
As a result of nurses’ partnership with the community and expanded  community outreach, the number of breast care consults at the Breast Care Center steadily increased from 60 in fourth quarter 2013 to an all-time high of 154 in fourth quarter 2015.

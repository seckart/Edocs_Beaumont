Organizational Overview 17

Exemplary Professional Practice

 

A description of the infrastructure, the organizational committees, and the decision-making bodies specifically designed to oversee the quality of patient care.

 

 

Beaumont Health Board of Directors

 

The Board of Directors for Beaumont Health assumes ultimate responsibility for the quality, safety and appropriateness of patient care provided. They have established and authorized a plan to provide the structure for performance improvement and patient safety.  

 

The Board recognizes performance improvement and patient safety to be an ongoing high priority for the health system. They have endorsed the Institute of Medicine’s definition of quality:

“Quality is the extent to which health services for individuals and populations increase the likelihood of desired health outcomes and are consistent with current professional knowledge.” 

To achieve this, a formal structure was developed to examine the care provided. This provides an opportunity to evaluate, monitor and improve the quality, safety and appropriateness of the care provided. (Evidence OO17-1, Beaumont Grosse Pointe Hospital Quality and Safety Communication Structure)

 

Quality and Safety Committee of the Board
The Board of Directors receives information from the Quality and Safety Committee of the Board (BQSC), which is a subcommittee of the Board. The BQSC provides strategic guidance and coordination of all quality and safety activities for the system. The data shared with the Board encompasses both internal and external quality and safety measures. The internal measures are sentinel event recommendations and the quality matrix, and the external measures include core measure and pay for performance scores and other publically reported measures.

 

Corporate Quality and Safety Leadership Team
The Quality and Safety Leadership Team (QSLT), a corporate committee, meets every month and reviews the system’s quality and safety data. It looks at core measure data, National Database of Nursing Quality Indicators (NDNQI), quality matrix data, sentinel event summary and patient satisfaction scores. Hospital-specific data for these areas presented on a rotating basis.

 

Members of the QSLT include corporate quality leadership and members from each hospital. Participants from Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) include the Director of Quality and Patient Safety, Patient Safety Officer, Hospital Vice President of Operations, Hospital Chief Nursing Officer and Hospital President. 

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe Quality and Safety Council
At the hospital level, the Quality Safety Council (QSC) meets monthly to review BGP-specific data and issues. Members include: 

  • Director of Quality, Safety and Epidemiology Department
  • Medical Chief of Quality and Safety and Patient Safety Officer
  • Hospital President
  • Physician-in-Chief
  • Chief Nursing Officer
  • Vice President of Operations
  • Chief of Medicine
  • Administrative Directors
  • Legal Affairs
  • Directors of Nursing
  • Administrative Managers
  • Nursing Leadership
  • System Quality and Safety Chief
  • Vice Presidents and Directors of Quality and Patient Safety

 

Each month, different departments present their quality matrix scores and action plans and the results of their culture of safety survey, which is a survey completed by direct patient care staff to measure their perception of safety. Action plans are formulated at the direct care personnel level, with leadership involvement, and are shared and reviewed at the Quality and Safety Council meetings. 

 

Every clinical department participates, and the focus is to identify areas for improvement in the hospital’s culture to make the environment safer for the patients and staff. It is encouraging for the staff to see these efforts and the attention paid to identified issues or concerns.

 

In addition to the monthly QSC meeting, there is a weekly rotation of Quality and Safety Teams. The rotating teams consist of nurses, process owners and nurse leaders from each clinical area, including Emergency Center, Trauma, Stroke Team, Critical Care, Medical/Surgical units, Mother Baby Unit, Surgical Service, Heart and Vascular, and Bariatric. The core group includes the Director for Quality and Safety, CNO, VP of Operations and Hospital President. The purpose of the weekly meeting is to address individual service area performance measures and barriers to performance, and to ensure actual process owners are engaged in the performance improvement efforts.

 

Additional monthly agenda items include the following:

  • Sentinel and high priority cases are reviewed, and the recommendations are approved or modified. 
  • NDNQI data are included in the matrix report. Areas not meeting target are presented, and associated action plans are reviewed and discussed.
  • The Infection Prevention Committee presents each month. It reports out previous meeting topics, areas of concern and any action plans related to hospital-wide infection prevention action plans, such as overall hand hygiene, MRSA lab events, etc. 
  • The Medical Executive Committee (MEC) updates are reported by the Patient Safety Officer. Updates include concerns from previous meetings and any significant changes made at the MEC.

 

The Quality and Safety Department nurses (Susan Muscat, BSN, RN, Director of Quality and Safety; Kelly Benoit, BSN, RN, Quality Nurse; and Lorraine Innes, RN, Quality Nurse) are involved in every aspect of quality of patient care. The quality nurses are champions for root cause analyses, sentinel and high priority reviews, and failure mode and effects analyses, as well as quality reviews and peer review for medical staff. 

 

Quality Information Disseminated to the Clinical Nurses
The Quality and Safety department reports directly to Vice President of Operations Kristen Cavender, MBA, BSIE, CSSBB, and has a dotted line to Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer. Cavender and Stewart attend the monthly QSC meetings and the MEC. Quality and Safety is also represented on the Professional Nurse Council (PNC) as a voting member. Benoit is the PNC Quality Department representative. Benoit and Muscat both present quality metrics and updates to the PNC on quality events, trends, recommendations and follow-up.  

 

The PNC is made up of clinical nurses. After they get information at the PNC, they take this back to their unit practice councils to the keep the rest of the clinical nurses informed.

 

Quality and Safety Rounding
Another way in which nursing and medical leadership demonstrate the commitment to safety and quality is through patient safety rounds, which are conducted monthly. The rounding team consists of the CNO, Patient Safety Officer, Quality and Safety Director, and a Board member.

 

The patient safety rounding team visits clinical areas and engages the staff in discussing what they deem to be safety concerns. These concerns are obstacles they encounter when trying to provide safe, quality and appropriate care to patients. While it is usually the nurses and nursing assistants who are interviewed during these rounds, anyone on the unit, including physicians, may be interviewed.

 

One of the mechanisms for process improvement in patient care is the multidisciplinary team approach used for serious safety events, sentinel events or high priority reviews. The root cause analysis methodology is used to determine system failures and identify resolutions. Serious quality or safety events are identified through a number of venues. 

 

Identifying Quality and Safety Issues
The most common method for identifying quality or safety concerns is through the electronic safety event reporting system, RL. Event reporting can be anonymous if the person making the report so chooses. The Patient Safety Nurse reviews and investigates every safety event report, doing so daily, and communicates these to nursing leaders and department leaders during the daily face to face operations meetings. Process owners work with department managers to identify and implement “quick fixes” or “gap actions” to address process or procedure failures. Feedback is given to the department manager to share with staff members as appropriate. The shared feedback helps staff members understand the value and positive improvement in patient safety and quality of care. 

 

Some events are beyond the quick fix and require the full multidisciplinary team approach, which generates the process for a root cause analysis. This is determined by Muscat and the Medical Chief of Quality and Safety. Actions taken to report and improve patient safety are recognized monthly with Eagle Eye awards and Good Catch recognition. The awards are presented during the monthly QSC meeting.

Organizational Overview 6
Transformational Leadership

 

The learner assessment of the continuing education needs for nurses at all levels and settings, and the related education implementation plan.

 

 

Two continuing education needs assessments were conducted in 2016:

  • The Grosse Pointe Nursing Needs Assessment was conducted in April 2016 and included Clinical Nurses, Education Specialists, Advanced Practice Nurses, Administrative Managers, Associate/Assistant Nurse Managers, Administrative Supervisors, Nursing Directors and the Chief Nursing Officer.
  • The Nurse Manager Inventory Tool was completed in November/December 2016 and included Administrative Managers and Directors.

 

The Grosse Pointe Nursing Needs Assessment was created and distributed at Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP). The Nurse Manager Inventory Tool was created by the Nurse Manager Leadership Partnership, which includes the American Organization of Nurse Executives (AONE) and the American Association of Critical-Care Nurses (AACN), and was distributed by BGP.

 

Neither survey tool contained questions that compromised participants’ anonymity. Flyers with information about the survey and instructions on how to participate were given to all BGP Clinical Nurses (RN), the CNO, Administrative Nurse Managers (AM), Associate Nurse Managers (ANM), Administrative Support, Clinical Nurse Specialists (CNS), Nurse Practitioners (NP) and Education Specialists (ES). All nursing staff members were encouraged to participate.

 

The Grosse Pointe Nursing Needs Assessment survey consisted of 13 questions in the generalized areas of:

  • Nurse Characteristics: Position, Nursing Education Level, Shift Worked and Area/Unit of Work
  • Continuing Education Program/Presentation: Preference of Length, Format, Location and Day of Week
  • Continuing Education Program Content: Preference of Area of Clinical Practice, Career Advancement, Research, Leadership/Staff Development and Areas of Personal Interest

 

Several questions allowed for multiple responses. There were a total of 360 respondents, which was a sizable sample as Beaumont Grosse Pointe employs 596 nurses (360/596, 60%).

The Nursing Education Department developed a program on Critical Thinking/Reasoning based on conversations with clinical nurses and nursing leadership. The Critical Thinking/Reasoning education was provided at the same time that the needs assessment was being distributed. As it turned out, critical thinking was one of the top areas the clinical nurses desired more education in.

  • (Evidence OO6-7 Presentation on Critical Thinking/Reasoning)  

 

The Nurse Manager Inventory Tool consisted of 81 questions in the generalized areas of:

  • Financial Management
  • Human Resource Management
  • Performance Improvement
  • Foundational Thinking Skills
  • Technology
  • Strategic Management
  • Appropriate Clinical Practice Knowledge
  • Human Resources Leadership Skills
  • Relationship Management and Influencing Behavior
  • Diversity
  • Shared Decision Making
  • Personal and Professional Accountability
  • Career Planning
  • Personal Journey Disciplines
  • Reflective Practice Reference Behaviors/Tenants

 

A total of 18 nursing leaders responded, representing 100% of the Nursing Administrative Managers and Directors. The CNO and Director of Professional Practice surveys were not included because they are the sponsors of the Leadership Series.

Organizational Overview 7

Structural Empowerment

 

An action plan that includes a target and demonstrates evidence of progress toward 80% of registered nurses obtaining a baccalaureate or graduate degree in nursing by 2020. Include an assessment of the current status; an evaluation of methods and strategies to increase the educational level of the workforce; and an appraisal of established, realistic targets to meet the organization's strategy to increase the number of registered nurses with a degree in nursing (baccalaureate or graduate degree).

 

 

Action Plan Target

 

80% of Beaumont Gross Pointe (BGP) registered nurses at all levels will have at least a baccalaureate (BSN) or graduate (MSN) degree in nursing by 2020. This includes clinical nurses, coordinators/supervisors, clinical nurse specialists/APRNs, directors/managers, executives and the CNO.

 

Assessment of the Current Status

 

Status of Progress Toward 80% of Nurses with a BSN or Higher Degree
Data on education level is collected either quarterly or annually in October to determine the percent of registered nurses at all levels who have a BSN or higher nursing degree.  The data for the following areas is collected quarterly and reported to NDNQI:

  • In-Patient Nursing
  • Surgical Services
  • Emergency Center

 

All other areas/services report the data annually to Randy Whitney, MSA, BSN, RN, NE-BC, Director of Professional Practice and MPD. The data is distributed quarterly to the CNO, directors and administrative managers and is then shared with the unit staffs.

 

BGP is committed to nurses’ ongoing education and development. Nursing leadership set a goal to increase nurses at all levels with a BSN or higher degree by 4-10% each year. The wide variability in the goal is due to programs put in place by BGP and Beaumont Health, as the anticipated benefit of the new and enhanced tuition reimbursement will not be evident until 2019 and 2020. The goal set by nursing leadership for 2017 is 60% of nurses with a BSN or higher, with 80% of nurses holding a BSN or higher by 2020.

Evaluation of Methods and Strategies

 

Strategy: Preferred Hiring of Candidates with a BSN
The Human Resources Department recruiters and administrative managers (AM) give preference to the applications of BSN-prepared candidates in the hiring process. AMs interview BSN-prepared nurses before nurses without a BSN. In 2015, BGP hired approximately 90 clinical nurses, 40-50% of whom were new graduates. The strategy to give preference to hiring nurses with a BSN will contribute to BGP achieving its 2017 goal.

 

Evaluation: The hiring of BSN and non-BSN nurses by unit, compiled from new hire data, is reviewed quarterly by hospital and nursing leadership.   

 

Strategy: Preferred Hiring of Canadian Nurses
BGP is located approximately 20 minutes from Windsor, Ontario Canada. Most Canadian nurses hired who have attended school in Canada within the last 10 years are bachelor’s-prepared, as the entry level for nurses in Ontario since 2005 has been a BSN. BGP attends numerous job fairs in Windsor, Ontario to recruit and hire BSN-prepared nurses.

 

Evaluation: BSN nurses hired from Canada, compiled from new hire data, is reviewed quarterly by nursing leadership.

 

Strategy: Professional Ladder Advancement Policy
The Professional Ladder advancement policy permits and encourages RNs whose primary role is to provide direct patient care to advance in clinical practice and receive recognition and compensation for their achievement and expertise. Clinical nurses receive points that may be claimed annually for credit toward Professional Ladder advancement. To become a Clinical Nurse IV, a minimum of a BSN degree is required. 
(Evidence OO5-11, Policy 245 Professional Ladder Program) (Evidence OO5-12, Policy 853 Professional Ladder Program)

 

Evaluation: The number of clinical nurses who have a BSN and are on the Professional Ladder, compiled by Nursing Education, is reviewed annually by nursing leadership.

 

Strategy: Degree Requirements
BGP sets expectations for formal nursing education by requiring certain degrees for specific job categories since June 2015. For example, a minimum of a BSN is required for many job categories in the organization, including chief nursing officer, director of nursing, administrative manager, associate nurse manager, clinical nurse specialist, educational specialist, care manager and nurse researcher.

 

Evaluation: The hiring of positions is monitored continually by Human Resources. Candidates who do not have at least a BSN are not eligible for hire/advancement. All nurses in these job categories hold at least a BSN.

 

Strategy: Enhanced Tuition Reimbursement for Nurses with a Diploma or Associate Degree to Obtain a BSN Degree
BGP nurses are benefiting from a new tuition reimbursement program to complete their BSN degree. As Beaumont Health supports the goal of 80% of nurses holding a BSN by 2020, it offers expanded tuition reimbursement for employees completing an RN to BSN degree. The new program, in place since December 2016 and available to both full- and part-time nurses, pays up to 70% of the average cost of a credit hour in Michigan (eight credits for full-time and four credits for part-time) per semester or quarter. As of January 2017, eight clinical nurses are enrolled in the program. (Evidence OO7-1, Tuition Reimbursement: RN to BSN Degree Completion)

 

In addition to the Enhanced Tuition Reimbursement program, the program providing $3,000 annually for education expenses is still available.

 

Evaluation: Annual expenditures for tuition reimbursement are increasing. This will include the number of clinical nurses enrolled in the program, the cost of the Enhanced Tuition Reimbursement and the percentage of BSN-prepared nurses. The dollars are budgeted at the corporate level. 

 

Strategy: Nursing Scholarships
Nurses receive scholarships to assist with continuing education expenses. Three nursing scholarships were distributed at BGP in 2016, two for undergraduate degrees and one for a graduate degree. The Beaumont Hospital Grosse Pointe Assistance League distributed $15,000 to undergraduate nursing students who were diploma or associate degree-prepared, as well as to entry level nursing students. The Francys D. Marco Scholarship distributed $6,000 to undergraduate nursing students who were diploma or associate degree-prepared, as well as to entry level nursing students. The Audrey N. Kottenstette scholarship distributed $750 to graduate nursing students who were employed as nurses at BGP.

 

Evaluation: The number and amount of scholarships given annually is determined by the interest on the endowments of each scholarship. Whitney and Nancy Jones, Manager, Gift Shop and Assistance League, are the hospital representatives who meet with representatives from the Assistance League and the Marco and Kottenstette families to assist in determining scholarship recipients.

 

Appraisal of Established, Realistic Targets

 

Goal to Achieve 80% of RNs with BSN or Higher by 2020
The goal to achieve 80% of nurses at all levels with a BSN is outlined in the graph below. The goal is to have 60% of all nurses at BGP hold at least a BSN degree in 2017, 65% in 2018, 70% in 2019 and 80% in 2020. BGP set this as a hospital-wide goal, and it is listed in the Employee Engagement sections of the Beaumont Grosse Pointe 2017 Objectives Monthly Strategic Tracker. With the programs currently in place and the importance of the hospital goal, full achievement of this target is expected.
(Evidence OO7-2, Beaumont Grosse Pointe 2017 Objectives Monthly Strategic Tracker)

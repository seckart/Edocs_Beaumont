Organizational Overview 11
Exemplary Professional Practice

 

A description of the process by which the CNO (or his or her designee) participates in credentialing, privileging, and evaluating advanced practice registered nurses (APRNs). Include the frequency of reprivileging.

 

 

Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer of Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP), is a member of the Beaumont Health System – Grosse Pointe Hospital Credentials and Qualification Committee. Stewart meets monthly with the Chief Medical Officer and Physician Chiefs to review and approve privileging and re-privileging of Advance Practice Nurses (APN). The APNs are Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetists (CRNA), Nurse Practitioners (NP) and Certified Nurse Midwives (CNM)

Credentialing and Privileging
The credentialing, privileging and re-privileging of APNs is initiated through the Beaumont Health (BH) Corporate Credentialing Office. Privileging is managed, monitored and tracked by this central location; re-privileging is on an every-two-years rotation based on the nurse’s birthday.

 

Advanced Practice Nurses’ applications for credentialing and privileging/re-privileging are sent to Stewart for review, approval and sign-off before being brought to the Credentials and Qualification Committee. Stewart returns incomplete applications to the Credentialing Office.

 

The credentialing, privileging and re-privileging process for BH-employed APNs is different from the process for private practice APNs. For BH-employed APNs, the employment application is processed through the Human Resources department, forwarded to the nurse leader and Physician Chief of the area that conducts the interview and then sent to the BH Corporate Credentialing Office for verification once the decision to make an offer has been made. Once the verification of education, training and experience is complete, the application is forwarded to the Chief of the department and then to Stewart for review and approval before being presented to the BGP Credentials and Qualification Committee.

 

For APNs who are employed through private practice physicians, the application for employment and the hiring is conducted through the private practice office. Once the APN is hired by the private physician practice, the required privileging information is sent to the BH Corporate Credentialing Office for validation and then to the sponsoring Physician Chief and Stewart for review and sign-off. 

 

Once reviewed and signed off on by Stewart, the APN paperwork is returned to the BH Corporate Credentialing Office for presentation at the Credentials and Qualification Committee meeting. There is an opportunity for discussion and final approval at the BGP Credentials and Qualification Committee meeting. Once reviewed and approved, the paperwork is returned to the Corporate Credentialing Office. 

 

Evaluation
Evaluations and appraisals of the hospital-employed APN’s are handled through Stewart’s designee, the nurse leader of that area. Questions or concerns about the performance of the APN are brought to Stewart for review and recommendations.  Employed APNs are given a performance appraisal once per year. 

 

Evaluation of non-hospital employed APNs is handled through their private practice. Concerns about the performance of a non-hospital employed APN are brought through the peer review process and Stewart works with the Physician Chief of the area involved. The concern and the results of the investigation are documented for reference during the re-privileging process.

Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP), part of Beaumont Health System, is a 250-bed, full-service acute care community-based hospital that offers medical, surgical, emergency, obstetric and critical care services. In 2015, there were 11,915 admissions, 841 births, 7,200 surgeries and 42,411 Emergency Center visits. BGP has 1,554 employees, 842 practicing physicians and 384 volunteers. Specialty services include the following:

  • The John and Marlene L. Boll Center for Human Development
  • The Beaumont Breast Care Center
  • The Janet A & H. Richard Freuhauf, Jr., Center for Orthopedic Medicine
  • Lung Nodule Clinic
  • Beaumont Vein Center
  • Integrative Medicine
  • Beaumont Sleep Center
  • Cough Clinic
  • The Elizabeth A. and G. John Stevens Center for Minimally Invasive Surgery

 

Hospital distinctions include:

  • Baby-Friendly designation by Baby-Friendly USA for the Cotton Family Birth Center in 2012
  • Joint Commission Advance Primary Stroke Center Certification
  • Society of Chest Pain Centers’ Chest Pain Center Accreditation
  • Level III Trauma Certification
  • Michigan’s first Diagnostic Imaging Center of Excellence, as designated by the American College of Radiology

 

2015 awards and rankings include:

  • Recognition as “high performing” in three medical specialties (Geriatrics, Orthopedics and Pulmonology) by U.S. News & World Report, 2016-2017.  Ranked #15 in Michigan, #9 in Metro Detroit (four-way tie)
  • Joint Commission Top Performer on Key Quality Measures for four consecutive years (2011, 2012, 2013, 2014)
  • Center of Distinction for Knee, Hip and Spine Surgery
  • U.S. News & World Report “Most Connected” Hospital, 2015-16.

 

Community involvement and partnerships: 

  • Beaumont Community Health Coalition
  • The Family Center
  • Neighborhood Club
  • Meals for the  Homebound
  • Beaumont Parenting Program
  • Cancer Survivorship Program
  • Project Healthy Schools

 

History
The hospital was originally known by the name Bon Secours Hospital. In 1924, a group of Bon Secours sisters came to the Detroit area to provide healthcare to the poor. Care was provided in the community, and in 1945 the construction of the hospital began. While it was a Catholic Hospital, it provided care to the entire community regardless of religion.

 

In 2006, the hospital was having financial difficulties, and in 2007 the keys of Bon Secours Hospital were handed over to Beaumont Health System, and the name was changed to Beaumont Hospital - Grosse Pointe.

 

In 2014, a new health system was formed by Beaumont Health System, Botsford Health Care and Oakwood Health Care, and named Beaumont Health.  Beaumont Health consists of eight hospitals with 3,337 beds, 168 outpatient sites, 5,000 physicians and 35,000 employees.

 

The hospital has an occupancy of 58% and serves as a site for the Family Medicine Residency Program from Oakland University William Beaumont School of Medicine. The hospital is located in the city of Grosse Pointe, in southeast Michigan. The Grosse Pointe community has a population of 45,000 and consists of five different cities: Grosse Pointe Parks, Grosse Pointe City, Grosse Pointe Farms, Grosse Pointe Woods and Grosse Pointe Shores. This is one of the most affluent suburban communities in Southeastern Michigan. 

 

Populations Served
The service area for BGP is the east side of Detroit, the Grosse Pointes and other eastern suburbs of Detroit. BGP serves people of different cultures and groups, including Eastern Europeans and African Americans. The population served is evenly divided between residents of the city of Detroit and the suburban area.

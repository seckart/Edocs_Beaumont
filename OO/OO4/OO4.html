Organizational Overview 4
Transformational Leadership

 

The administrative and nursing organizational chart(s). Describe the CNO's structural and operational relationships to all areas in which nursing is practiced.

 

 

The Hospital Administration Organization Chart 300 shows the relationships that Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer, has within Beaumont Grosse Pointe. (Evidence OO4-1, Hospital Organizational Chart) Stewart reports directly to the Hospital President and Physician in Chief, as do the rest of the Hospitals Vice Presidents and Administrative Directors. Stewart’s responsibilities include the operational oversight of several departments, nursing and non-nursing, which is clearly defined in the CNO positions definition.

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe’s Centers of Excellence (COE) comprise seven clinical specialty areas within the Health System, and Stewart has direct responsibilities for three of the COEs. The organization chart reflects that Stewart has both direct and indirect dotted line reporting relationships for all nurses at Beaumont Grosse Pointe and is accountable for all areas of nursing.   

 

The System Nurse Executive Council is the all-encompassing leadership presence for nursing throughout Beaumont Health. The meeting is considered critical for the communication of information, identification of issues or concerns, and development of the strategic plan for nursing.  The Beaumont Grosse Pointe Nurse Executive Council is the body that further defines nursing strategies specific to Beaumont– Grosse Pointe.

 

The Nursing Administration Organization Chart shows all the direct and indirect relationships of nurses in areas that report to the CNO and other Vice Presidents and Administrative Directors. (Evidence OO4-2, Nursing Organizational Chart) It further shows the director’s responsibility for the practice of nursing in the hospital inpatient setting and the structural relationship with the other areas of nursing. Operationally, the CNO represents all nurses at the highest-level decision-making meeting.  The chain of command flows from the direct care nurse to the CNO.

 

Stewart connects with all nurses personally through rounding, drop in meetings, her open door policy and the Professional Nurse Council.

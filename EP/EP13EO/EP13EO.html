Interprofessional Care EP13EO

 

Nurses participate in interprofessional groups that implement and evaluate coordinated patient education activities.

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, of an interprofessional patient education activity that was associated with an improved patient outcome. Supporting evidence must be submitted in the form of a graph with a data table that clearly displays the data.

 

 

Decreasing Length of Stay for 3 South Orthopaedic Spinal Surgery Patients – “Back on Track”Class

 

Background/Problem
Poor surgical preparation can cause patients and families anxiety about their pending surgery and treatment plan. A lack of adequate preoperative education and discharge planning can also result in a longer length of stay and negative outcomes such as infection, deep vein thrombosis, urinary tract infection, mobility issues and other postoperative complications.

 

Cathleen Solecki, MSN, RN, CCRN, CNRN, Program Manager Neurology, represents Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) at the Beaumont Health System Corporate Neurological Centers of Excellence Committee (COE). The objectives of the Neuro COE include sharing best practices, streamlining and standardizing treatment practices and driving process improvement.  

 

At the September 2013 Neuro COE meeting, the committee discussed the length of stay (LOS) on the BGP 3 South Orthopaedic unit and identified an opportunity to decrease it.

 

Solecki scheduled a meeting with Alexis Rogers, RN, ONC, Orthopaedic Assistant Nurse Manager, and Lisa Hartner, Orthopaedic Joint Coordinator, to discuss the COE’s concerns and the need to decrease LOS on the unit. The Orthopaedic unit’s LOS was higher than projected according to one of the benchmarks the COE uses, the Michigan Spine Surgery Improvement Collaborative (MSSIC). The MSSIC is a statewide quality improvement collaborative involving orthopedic surgeons and neurosurgeons, with the aim of improving the quality of care for spine surgery. The objective of this collaborative is to improve patient care outcomes while increasing the efficiency of treatment.

 

The COE instructed BGP to develop a process improvement to decrease the LOS for spine surgery patients.

 

Rogers, Solecki and Hartner brainstormed ideas, and Rogers suggested that a program similar to the BGP Total Joint Program (TJP) might decrease the LOS for spine surgery patients. The TJP is an interprofessional education program for patients undergoing total joint replacements. It had been instituted four years earlier and dramatically decreased the LOS and improved patient satisfaction in the total joint replacement patient population. Rogers, Solecki and Hartner agreed to try a program like this since it had had a positive effect for joint patients. 

 

In November 2013, Rogers met with Phyllis Reynolds, MSN, RN, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer at the time, to obtain approval. Rogers, Solecki and Hartner determined that an interprofessional group was needed to develop, implement and facilitate the program. This group, the Spine Patient Education Committee, comprised the following disciplines:

  • Nursing
  • Care Management
  • Physical Therapy
  • Occupational Therapy

 

Solecki and Hartner agreed to take the lead on developing the program to decrease the 3 South Orthopaedic unit’s spine surgery patient length of stay. The team reviewed the data, which showed that the average LOS for spinal surgery patients on 3 South Orthopaedic was 3.06 days in fourth quarter 2013.

 

Goal Statement
To decrease the average length of stay for spinal surgery patients, measured in days, for 3 South Orthopaedic.

 

Description of the Intervention/Initiative/Activities
January 2014
The Spine Patient Education Committee developed the goal of decreasing the LOS for 3 South Orthopaedic spinal surgery patients. The committee included members from nursing, Care Management, Physical Therapy, Occupational Therapy and Hospital Administration.

 

After much discussion, the committee decided to develop a preoperative spinal education program for all patients coming to BGP for spinal surgery. The program would be similar to the Total Joint Program. 

 

The Spine Patient Education Committee met to develop the program. The overall goal was to provide an education activity for spinal surgery patients covering the entire surgical experience, from preoperative education to discharge planning.

 

The committee reviewed the Total Joint Program and determined which topics could also be used for the spine patient education. Solecki and Hartner met with Rogers from the Orthopedic units; Jill Phillips, MPT, Physical Therapist; Michele Sucharski, MPT, Manager, Occupational Therapy; and Judith Sudomier, RN, Care Manager, Care Management, to discuss the program outline. 

 

The program, called Back on Track, was developed based on the input of the four disciplines and designed to be similar to the Total Joint Program, but with a focus on spinal surgery patients. 

 

The Back on Track class outline included the following topics:

Nursing

  • How to prepare for surgery
  • Spinal Anatomy
  • Spinal Procedures
  • Infection Prevention
  • Before your Surgery
  • During your Surgery
  • After your Surgery
  • Hospital Stay
  • Integrative Therapies such as music, aroma and massage
  • Pain Management
  • Wound Care

 

Physical Therapy

  • Home Preparation
  • In-Patient Therapy
  • Out-Patient Therapy

 

Occupational Therapy

  • Home Preparation
  • In-Patient Therapy
  • Out-Patient Therapy

 

Care Management

  • Discharge Planning.

 

The classes, to be held at Beaumont Grosse Pointe every Monday from 3 p.m. to 4:30 p.m., would consist of an interactive discussion along with a PowerPoint presentation.  The classes were not mandatory, but strongly encouraged.

 

After the committee developed the program, it asked Patricia Jax, BSN, RN, clinical nurse who was in charge of the Total Joint Program, to review it and make suggestions for improvement. After discussing it with Reynolds, it was decided that Jax would also teach the class.

 

February 2014
A flyer was developed to be delivered to all orthopedic physicians who perform surgery at BGP. The flyer reviews the objectives of the Back on Track class, the topics covered and how to register.

 

The committee worked on the preoperative protocol for the surgeon, hospital and pre-admission testing. A work flow was developed to ensure that patients were getting the proper pre-procedure pathway so they would be appropriately educated before coming to surgery. It was important that all protocol be followed, as the steps in the protocol were discussed in the classes. The work flow consisted of the following steps:

 

Pre-op Protocol for Surgeon’s Office

  • Physician distributes chlorhexidine to the patients
  • Physician gives a prescription for a nasal swab to check for MRSA
  • Physician distributes flyer and encourages attendance at the Back on Track class

 

Pre-op Protocol for Hospital

  • The scheduled surgical patients are contacted by Jax, who invites them to a Back on Track class and encourages attendance. If they are unable to attend, she will mail the educational materials to the patient and family.
  • The pre-op laboratory work that is required  for surgery is reviewed, and the patients are encouraged to the complete these tests on the day of the Back on Track class.

 

Pre-op Protocol for Pre-Admission Testing

  • The pre-admission nurses contact patients to discuss pre-admission testing requirements and review the schedule for showering with chlorhexidine prior to surgery.

 

March 2014
All work for the Back on Track program was completed and the classes began.

EP13EO, Participants

 

Outcomes
After developing and implementing an interprofessional preoperative educational program called Back on Track, the average length of stay for 3 South Orthopaedic spinal surgery patients decreased from 3.06 days in fourth quarter 2013 to 2.14 days in fourth quarter 2014.

Professional Practice Model EP1

 

Clinical nurses are involved in the development, implementation, and evaluation of the professional practice model.

  • New applicants: Provide a description, with supporting evidence, of the development of the nursing professional practice model and how clinical nurses were involved.
  • Redesignating applicants: Provide a description, with supporting evidence, of the ongoing evaluation of the nursing professional practice model and how clinical nurses are involved.

 

 

 

New Applicant: Development of the Professional Practice Model

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP), part of Beaumont Health System, is a 250-bed, full-service acute care community-based hospital that offers medical, surgical, emergency, obstetric and critical care services. In 2015, there were 11,915 admissions, 841 births, 7,200 surgeries and 42,411 Emergency Center visits. BGP has 1,554 employees, 842 practicing physicians and 384 volunteers.

 

In 2014, a new health system was formed by Beaumont Health System, Botsford Health Care and Oakwood Health Care, and named Beaumont Health. Beaumont Health consists of eight hospitals with 3,337 beds, 168 outpatient sites, 5,000 physicians and 35,000 employees. BGP is part of Beaumont Health.

 

Prior to the formation of Beaumont Health, each health system had a Professional Practice Model (PPM). Beaumont Health System’s model was the Beaumont PPM, based on a Caring model with Beaumont Nurses being “VIP” (vision, innovation and professional practice). The schematic is shown below.

 

 

 

EP1, Beaumont PPM

 

 

In December 2015, Susan Grant, DNP, RN, NEA-BC, FAAN started in her role as the Executive Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer (CNO) at Beaumont Health. In her role as the new CNO, one of the major initiatives was to blend the three nursing divisions from Beaumont, Botsford and Oakwood into one division of Beaumont Health.

 

The development of the PPM was high on Grant’s list of tasks that needed to be completed within the first year. The development of a PPM would assist in bringing down the barriers between the three individual systems and in beginning to form a new nursing division of Beaumont Health.

 

The CNOs of the eight hospitals determined that a retreat would be the best way to facilitate the development of the PPM. The retreat would include nurses from all eight hospitals and the ambulatory setting. The attendees included clinical nurses, Assistant Nurse Managers, Administrative Managers, Clinical Nurse Specialists, Directors of Nursing and Chief Nursing Officers.

 

The Professional Practice Model Retreat was scheduled for June 29 and 30, 2016, with the following topics on the agenda:

  • Defining the PPM
  • How a PPM related to Beaumont Health
  • Patient and Family Centered Care
  • Reduction of Suffering through Compassionate Connected Care
  • Integrative Nurse Coaching
  • Review top five values and tabulate results
  • Professional practice model development
  • Determining the top 3 PPM Graphics
    (Evidence EP1-1, Professional Practice Model Retreat Agenda, June 29 and 30, 2016)

PPM Development
The retreat was scheduled for two days. The first day was designed to provide basic knowledge regarding a PPM, and the second day was for the development of the PPM. One of the tasks assigned to the group was to identify the top five values of the Beaumont nursing community.

 

Nurses were assigned to a group, and each group discussed and came to a consensus about the top five values for the group. Grant provided the groups with a list of values, but it was also made clear that write-ins would be acceptable. The consensus of the 275 nurses at the retreat was that Beaumont Nurses should promote the following five values:

  • Advocacy
  • Caring
  • Environment
  • Professional Development
  • Safety

 

After the top five values were identified, discussion continued to determine whether these were the correct values. After a lengthy discussion, the following five values were agreed upon by the retreat participants as best describing a Beaumont Nurse:

  • Advocacy
  • Caring
  • Healing Environment
  • Professional Practice
  • Safety

 

They also determined through discussion that the patient and family, as well as Beaumont Health’s Values, should be represented on the PPM. (Evidence EP1-3, List of Beaumont Nurse Values)

 

For the next part of the day, each group was tasked with developing a schematic drawing of a new PPM incorporating the five values of the Beaumont Nurse. After the groups completed their schematic designs, a representative from each group gave a brief description of their design. The participants were then given three dots and instructed to put one on each of the top three designs that they believed best represented the Beaumont Nurse.

 

The top three designs were chosen at the retreat and then sent to Beaumont marketing to develop a professional looking PPM. (Evidence EP1-4, Designs of the PPM)

 

Input from all Clinical Nurses and Nursing Leadership
To ensure that all nurses had a voice in the selection of the Beaumont Health PPM, a viewing gallery consisting of the top five graphics from the retreat was planned for October 11–20, 2016. (The CNOs of the eight Beaumont Health hospitals had decided to include the fourth and fifth designs as well since they were only one point behind the third design in the voting.)

 

Posters of each graphic were displayed in the private dining room at BGP for nurses to review at designated times. Each nurse was asked to fill out a PPM viewing gallery feedback form on which they ranked the five graphics in order of preference and commented on what they liked most and least about the graphics. (Evidence EP1-5, PPM Viewing Gallery Information Sheet and Feedback Form)

 

In December 2016, after voting by over 1,700 Beaumont Health nurses, a new Professional Practice Model design was selected for the Nurses at Beaumont Health.  See OO8 for the chosen design of the PPM for the Nurses at Beaumont Health.

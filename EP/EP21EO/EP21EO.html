Culture of Safety EP21EO

 

Nurses are involved in implementing and evaluating national or international patient safety goals.

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, of nurses' involvement in activities that address national or international patient safety goals that led to an improvement in patient safety outcomes. Supporting evidence must be submitted in the form of a graph with a data table the clearly displays the data.

 

 

Increasing Notification of Critical Troponin Values to the Provider within 60 Minutes

 

Background/Problem
The communication of critical test results is an important element of daily nursing, although the accurate and timely documentation of the receipt of these results can be a challenge in the midst of a hectic daily routine. The importance of this task, however, cannot be overstated, and it relates directly to the 2015 & 2016 National Patient Safety Goal (NPSG). Under the heading of “Improve Staff Communication,” NPSG.02.03.01 directs us to “Get important test results to the right staff person on time.”

 

The objective of NPSG 02.03.01 is to report critical test results to the correct provider and implement appropriate actions if needed. According to Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) policy, this reporting must be done within 60 minutes.

 

The current process is for the testing department to notify the clinical nurse of a critical test value. The nurse then notifies the appropriate provider of the critical results and documents the notification and any orders, if given, in the electronic medical record (EMR).

 

In November 2015, a Mock Joint Commission visit carried out by internal quality personnel from Beaumont Health revealed that this process was flawed, as there was no consistent method of documentation and the process varied widely.

 

The documentation of critical values was revised so that nurses could document a critical lab note through the Shift Navigator and include a note designating it as a “Critical Test Result.” 

 

A January 2016 audit of troponin critical values was completed on the Critical Care Stepdown Unit (CCSU), and it showed that 71% of the results were relayed to the appropriate provider within the 60 minute time frame. 

 

Goal Statement
To increase the percentage of troponin critical test results on the CCSU reported to the provider within 60 minutes. 

 

Description of the Intervention/Initiative/Activities
This was considered a high priority requires due to the urgent nature of the issue, since it was identified on a Mock Joint Commission visit. In February 2016, Angela Falzarano, MSN, RN-BC, Clinical Nurse Specialist, took the issue to the oneChart Corporate Nursing Review Committee (oCNRC). This committee decided that the most effective way to make this change would be to move documentation to the Flowsheets section of Epic and create pull downs with cascade information. 

 

Kathy Grady, BSN, RN, Computer Specialist, created “Critical Test Results” tab that involves documenting the following information:

  • Name of person giving results
  • Critical test result
  • Action taken
  • Date contacted
  • Time contacted
  • Provider name
  • Provider response
  • Date provider responded
  • Time provider responded

 

This was done so that the single entry in the EMR would provide all the necessary information. Once the build was completed and tested, IT Informatics worked with Nursing Education to provide a series of screen shots of the old method and the updated method, to be used for educational purposes. 

 

  • February 15–March 22, 2016: Falzarano and Deb Briske, MSN-Ed, RN, CEN organized education on the new process being rolled out, to be provided in nursing education forums and as one-on-one education for the clinical nurses.  Clinical nurses were also educated to no longer use any note work flow in documenting Critical Test Results.
  • March 22, 2016: All sites and involved areas were notified of the go-live.  Flowsheet documentation was set to go into production.
  • March 24, 2016: oCNRC recommended adding the flowsheet to all nursing navigators, including procedural areas.
  • March 29, 2016: oCNRC reconvened and confirmed the changes and live production of the new Critical Test Results notification system set for April 5, 2016.
  • April 4, 2016: The build was complete and sent to production.
  • April 5, 2016: The Critical Test Results flowsheet went live in Epic.

Outcomes
Nurses’ involvement in implementing a Joint Commission National Patient Safety Goal improved patient safety outcomes. The notification of critical troponin values to the provider within 60 minutes on CCSU increased from 71% in January 2016 to 92% in July 2016.

EP22EO

 

Culture of Safety EP22EO

 

Unit- or clinic-level nurse-sensitive clinical indicator data outperform the mean or median of the national database used.

For an acute care organization with or without ambulatory/outpatient services, six nurse-sensitive clinical indicators are required. The required indicators for all acute care organizations include falls with injury, hospital-acquired pressure ulcers stages 2 and above, central line-associated bloodstream infection, and catheter-associated urinary tract infection. The remaining two nurse-sensitive clinical indicators must be selected from the list in Table 5 [page 52 of the manual]. For organizations with ambulatory/outpatient services only, two nurse-sensitive clinical indicators are required, which must be selected from the list in Table 5.

 

Organizations seeking Magnet designation must submit the following:

  • Provide nationally benchmarked unit- or clinic-level nurse-sensitive clinical indicator data using tables and graphs to display 8 quarters of data for the 30 months before documentation submission. Table 5 outlines the required nurse-sensitive clinical indicators for:
    • Acute care organizations with ambulatory/outpatient settings
    • Acute care organizations without ambulatory/outpatient settings
    • Organizations with only ambulatory/outpatient settings.
  • Use national databases when available. If a national database is not available, the organization must demonstrate that internal benchmarks are based on professional standards, literature review, or internal trended data, or all three.

 

For system applications, refer to system requirements in Appendix C.

 

 

 

Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP) tracks nurse-sensitive indicators using the National Database of Nursing Quality Indicators (NDNQI). The benchmark used for comparison is the Magnet Mean.
